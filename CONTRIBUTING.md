# Contributing

Each document in this repository has its own author list and its own status, noted at the top of the file. Contributions of every size are welcome; here is how they are handled.

## Corrections and small improvements

Typos, broken references, clearer phrasings, missing citations: open a pull request directly. Merged corrections are acknowledged in the relevant document.

## Substantial contributions

Progress on an open problem, a new result, a counterexample, a new section or worked example: **open an issue first** to discuss it before investing serious work. This protects you from duplicating effort or working at cross purposes with the authors, and it puts the coauthorship question on the table from the start.

## Coauthorship

A substantial contribution to a document — new results, resolution or significant sharpening of an open problem, major new exposition — earns an invitation to join that document's author list. This applies at every rung of the ladder: helping a research problem grow into an agenda, or an agenda into a draft paper, is exactly the kind of contribution that makes a coauthor.

What counts as substantial is judged per document by its current authors, in conversation with the contributor, and settled *before* the contribution is merged, not after. When in doubt, ask in an issue; erring on the side of asking costs nothing. Coauthorship applies to the specific document touched, not to the repository as a whole.

## Proposing a new problem

If you have an open problem that fits the collection — small enough to start, close enough to AI safety to matter — open an issue describing it. If it fits, we will work it into shape together.

## Writing math in Markdown

GitHub renders LaTeX in `.md` files: `$...$` inline and `$$...$$` for displays. For a display that renders incorrectly, a fenced ` ```math ` block is more robust; escape literal dollar signs as `\$`.
